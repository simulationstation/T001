Mission:

Working scientific posture:
- The analysis is assumption-conditioned, not assumption-free.
- Treat failed-supernova phenomenology as an export-failure / branch-selection problem.
- Expect a soft branch surface rather than a single hard explosion/failure threshold.
- Preserve intermediate cases: partial suppression, fallback-heavy outcomes, weak or ambiguous transients, near-silent disappearances.
- Do not relax detector rigor because of this framing.

Non-negotiable detector discipline:
1. Detect on difference images, not source catalogs alone.
2. Register images empirically from stars, not nominal WCS alone.
3. Perform forced photometry at the same sky location across epochs, including low-S/N and non-detection cases.
4. Carry explicit provenance for overlap, chip-gap, mask, edge, and no-coverage outcomes.
5. Keep the blinded known-supernova benchmark as the standing regression test.
6. Do not escalate a candidate scientifically if benchmark recovery regresses or provenance becomes less explicit.

How to think about survivors:
- A large fade by itself is not the target.
- The target is a residual that survives registration sanity checks, subtraction sanity checks, provenance checks, and same-location forced-photometry checks.
- Rank candidates by export-failure coherence, not only by amplitude.
- Penalize dipoles, centroid migration, low anchor counts, unstable local fields, obvious wavelength-dependent dust patterns, and late rebrightening.

When extending code:
- Prefer additive, inspectable columns and explicit score terms over opaque model outputs.
- Extend existing CLI and output patterns instead of inventing disconnected side paths.
- Write machine-readable parquet/csv/json outputs plus a human-readable markdown report.
- Keep score components traceable to actual measurements already produced by the repo.
- If a desired feature cannot be computed cleanly from current artifacts, document the missing prerequisite instead of inventing precision.

When changing ranking logic:
- Benchmark recovery is the external brake. Do not tune ranking in a way that weakens or hides benchmark failures.
- Provenance ambiguity must lower confidence, not merely appear in a note.
- Intermediate branch-like candidates are allowed, but they must still survive anti-artifact controls.
- The correct order is detector realism first, candidate interpretation second, publication claims last.

What future work should usually do:
1. Run the strict detector or reuse an existing strict output tree.
2. Run branch-aware scoring against that strict tree.
3. Compare new rankings against the blind benchmark context and pair-level provenance.
4. Follow only the top surviving branch-ranked objects with deeper forced photometry, SED work, or external vetting.
5. Update README and reports only after the detector and branch-aware outputs agree.

What not to do:
- Do not recast ordinary fades as “export failures” just because they are interesting.
- Do not trust catalog disappearances without residual-image support.
- Do not treat dust/systematic competitors as optional caveats.
- Do not use the assumptions as permission to weaken registration, subtraction, or benchmark rigor.
